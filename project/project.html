IS EUROPE'S ENERGY ADDICTION IMPROVING?

Energy consumption is a massive part of a country’s economy. And now, with climate change being a center point for many policymakers, its important to look at energy consumption’s role in climate change and how its likely to change in the future. To do this, comparisons between European countries and their trends over time will used, with the aim of answering whether they are on track to curb their energy usage.

Specifically, this project aims to answer the following:
- How do countries compare in their energy consumption, energy production, and energy emissions.
- How are the primary influences of final energy consumption likely to change in the future.
- Evaluate which countries have reduced their energy consumption most in the past few years.

What influences energy consumption?

The biggest drivers behind recent changes in European energy consumption are activity levels and geographic factors (S. Thomas, J. Rosenow). Indicators for these are as follows:
- Economic growth, an indicator for activity levels in Industry.
- Total passenger and freight-tonne kilometers traveled, an indicator of activity levels in the transportation industry.
- Employment rate, an indicator for activity levels in the service industry.
- Average winter temperatures, an indicator for residential energy demand which is mostly for heating homes.
- Population, an indicator for the long-term trend of energy demand in a country.
